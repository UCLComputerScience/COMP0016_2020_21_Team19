The Problem

NHS clinicians have no reliable, centralised mechanism to gather Patient Reported Outcome Measures (PROMs) per individual and to understand the factors that help and hinder an individual from adherence to set treatment plans. This problem doesn't just exist in the NHS. Reported outcomes and subsequent analysis are a vital feedback mechanism in numerous disciplines, from classrooms to hospital wards.

Slow

PROMs are typically collected by mailing paper questionnaires to patients. The NHS reports that for a single group of patients, PROMs data can take up to a year to collect.

Difficult to analyse

Collecting responses of physical questionnaires means manually updating records - providing no quick and easy visualisations to assist in analysing the repsonse data.

Necessary

It is becoming increasingly important for doctors to continue monitoring the health of their patients after they’ve been discharged. Many hospitals are now mandated to do so.

Our Solution

Web-based solution to capturing user-reported outcomes

  • Clinicians can assign tasks to groups
  • Patients can report the outcomes of these tasks
  • Progress visualisations provided for both clinicians and patients
Learn More

Collect & Analyse Results

  • Submit New Tasks
    Completely digitize the process of distributing PROMs and collecting their responses.
  • Gather Responses
    Instantly receive and visualise both quantitative and qualitative responses for a given task.
  • Visualise Results
    Results are aggregated per group, giving clinicians the ability to analyse progress for both individuals and groups as a whole.

Generalised Platform

  • Invitation Only
    Aside those creating an Organisation on the app for the first time it is invite-only, ensuring only those invited to your organisation can see those within it.
  • Single Sign-On Support
    With SSO extensibility provided out of the box, adding just a few lines of code can allow users to sign in with a range of different providers.
  • User Management
    Clinicians can choose to invite patients to the platform individually, or bulk import using Microsoft Excel spreadsheets.
